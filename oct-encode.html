This is a minimal example of mesh compression via vertex attribute quatization and oct-encoded normals. The positions and normals of the sphere on the left are encoded as standard 32-bit floats. The positions of the sphere on the right are quantized to 16-bit unsigned ints and the normals are oct-encoded. These techniques reduce the size of the mesh from kB to kB (%). Since the mesh is decoded on the fly on the GPU, the savings will affect transmission time, memory footprint, and GPU bus usage.

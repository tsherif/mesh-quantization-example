This is a minimal example of mesh compression via vertex attribute quatization and oct-encoded normals. The positions and normals of the sphere on the left are encoded as standard 32-bit floats. The positions of the sphere on the right are quantized to a 16-bit unsigned int per component and the normals are oct-encoded into 2 bytes. A total of 8 bytes are used to represent each vertex rather than the standard 24 (66% compression). Since the mesh is decoded on-the-fly on the GPU, the savings will affect transmission time, memory footprint, and GPU bus usage.
